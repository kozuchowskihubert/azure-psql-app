2 CREATING A TB-303 BASSLINE

Add the TB-303 Module

The TB-303 is the heart of acid techno. Here's how to set it up:

  • Open the Module Browser (click "+" or press M)
  • Find "TB-303 Acid Bass" in the Synthesizers category
  • Drag it to your workspace
  • Connect the output to the Master mixer

Programming the Pattern

  • Cutoff - Start around 50%, sweep for classic acid sound
  • Resonance - Set to 70% for that squelchy character
  • Accent - Add emphasis to specific steps
  • Slide - Enable glide between notes

3 TR-909 DRUM PROGRAMMING

Classic Techno Drum Pattern

Build a driving techno beat with these essential elements:

  • Kick - Steps 1, 5, 9, 13 (four-on-the-floor)
  • Snare/Clap - Steps 5, 13 (backbeat)
  • Hi-Hat Closed - All off-beats (2, 4, 6, 8...)
  • Hi-Hat Open - Step 8, 16 (for movement)

Sound Design Tips

  • Tune the kick drum to match your bassline key
  • Add subtle swing (5-15%) for groove
  • Layer rides and cymbals for texture

Connecting Cables

  • Click on an output jack (right side of module)
  • Drag to an input jack (left side of destination)
  • Cable color indicates signal type (audio = orange, CV = blue)
  • Right-click a cable to delete it

5 EFFECTS & MIXING

Essential Effects for Techno

  • Delay - Sync to BPM, use dotted 8th notes for groove
  • Reverb - Short room for drums, longer for pads
  • Distortion - Add grit to bass and drums
  • Compression - Glue your mix together

Mix Levels

  • Keep the kick prominent (-6dB to -4dB)
  • Bass should support, not overpower (-8dB to -6dB)
  • Leave headroom for mastering (-6dB peak)
